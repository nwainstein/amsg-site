Research Areas

Advancing next-generation AI hardware by bridging device, circuit, and system innovations

Holistic framework combining analog/mixed-signal computing, in-memory architectures, and ultra-high-speed interconnects.

Time-Domain Computing

Design of mixed-signal computational primitives that encode information in delay, pulse width, phase, or timing trajectories. This direction connects data conversion, low-energy processing, and hardware-efficient machine learning.

  • Time-domain arithmetic and signal representation
  • TDC/VTC/DLL-inspired building blocks
  • Calibration, PVT robustness, and implementation scalability

In-Memory Computing

Architectures and macros that reduce data movement by co-locating storage and computation. Emphasis is placed on mixed-signal interfaces, time-domain readout, non-volatile devices, and algorithm-aware tradeoffs.

  • FeFET / NVM-compatible compute fabrics
  • Crossbar-inspired and CAM-based macro concepts
  • Energy efficiency, precision, and array-level integration

AI Hardware Accelerators

Custom accelerator concepts for ANN and neuromorphic workloads, bridging circuits and system-level efficiency. The site can later expand this section with project pages, test chips, and benchmark summaries.

  • Edge-oriented and energy-aware hardware
  • Hybrid analog/digital acceleration
  • Macro-to-system integration strategies

Ultra-Dense D2D & C2C Links

Short-reach transceivers and clocking techniques for chiplet and advanced-package systems. Research includes signaling efficiency, interface constraints, and link architectures suited to dense integration.

  • Inverter-based and low-swing short-reach links
  • Clocking, forwarding strategies, and equalization tradeoffs
  • Design-for-test and package-aware constraints
